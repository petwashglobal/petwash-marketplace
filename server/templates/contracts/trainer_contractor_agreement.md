## 1. SCOPE OF TRAINING SERVICES

### 1.1 Training Programs
The Contractor agrees to provide professional pet training services ("Services") including:
- **Basic Obedience:** Sit, stay, come, heel, leash training
- **Behavioral Modification:** Aggression, anxiety, fear, separation issues
- **Advanced Training:** Off-leash control, agility, tricks, specialized tasks
- **Puppy Socialization:** Critical period development (8-16 weeks)
- **Service/Therapy Dog Preparation:** Task training, public access certification
- **Group Classes:** Multi-pet group training sessions
- **Private Sessions:** One-on-one customized training

### 1.2 Service Locations
Training services may be provided at:
- Client homes
- Pet Wash™ training facilities
- Public outdoor spaces (parks, trails, etc.)
- Pet-friendly commercial spaces
- Virtual sessions (video consultations)

### 1.3 Professional Standards
The Contractor shall:
- Use positive reinforcement training methods exclusively (no aversive tools)
- Maintain current certifications and continuing education
- Follow ethical guidelines of recognized training organizations
- Provide customized training plans for each client
- Track progress and provide written reports
- Communicate clearly with clients and Pet Wash™ coordinators

### 1.4 Independent Contractor Status
The Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee. The Contractor:
- Controls training methods and curriculum
- Sets own schedule (within client availability)
- Provides own training equipment and supplies
- Pays own taxes and contributions
- Is free to accept or decline training assignments

## 4. TRAINING METHODS & ETHICS

### 4.1 Humane Training Standards
The Contractor agrees to:
- Use **positive reinforcement ONLY** (rewards-based training)
- **PROHIBITED:** Shock collars, prong collars, choke chains, physical corrections, alpha rolls, dominance-based methods
- Follow LIMA principles (Least Intrusive, Minimally Aversive)
- Prioritize animal welfare above training speed
- Educate clients on humane training philosophy

### 4.2 Recognized Ethical Guidelines
The Contractor adheres to ethical standards of:
- Association of Professional Dog Trainers (APDT)
- Certification Council for Professional Dog Trainers (CCPDT)
- International Association of Animal Behavior Consultants (IAABC)
- Karen Pryor Academy (KPA)

### 4.3 Safety Protocols
The Contractor shall:
- Assess dog temperament before starting training
- Refuse unsafe assignments (high-bite-risk dogs without proper setup)
- Use secure training environments (fenced areas, controlled spaces)
- Carry pet first aid kit and know emergency veterinary locations
- Maintain liability waiver signed by all clients
